Water Injection Dosing Program

Supported early-stage development of a chemical dosing program for offshore water injection wells, aimed at mitigating corrosion risks and improving long-term well integrity.

Project Overview

Supported the early-stage development of a chemical dosing program for offshore water injection wells. The project aimed to mitigate corrosion risks and improve long-term well integrity by evaluating biocide treatment strategies during planned and unplanned shut-in periods.

The Problem

Inspection data from pulled tubing revealed elevated corrosion in water injection wells, with localized pitting linked to extended shut-in periods. Stagnant conditions promoted microbiologically influenced corrosion (MIC), requiring a dosing strategy to reduce long-term integrity risk and extend asset life.

My Contributions

  • Reviewed tubing inspection data to assess corrosion trends and likely failure mechanisms
  • Researched MIC and potential mitigation approaches
  • Coordinated with vendors to gather technical data, testing results, and safety information
  • Worked with specialized contractors to evaluate biocide options based on performance, compatibility, operational fit, and cost
  • Supported cost analysis and drafted initial dosing recommendations
  • Identified next steps for implementation and ongoing performance monitoring, providing a framework for a structured dosing program

Final Outcome / Impact

  • Established framework for a structured water injection dosing program
  • Identified the most effective biocide option through comparative technical evaluation
  • Provided comparative analysis to support informed chemical selection
  • Developed cost evaluation to guide operational planning
  • Outlined a clear implementation plan covering product selection, dosing criteria, and ongoing performance monitoring

This project strengthened asset integrity planning and laid the groundwork for reactive corrosion mitigation in offshore operations.
